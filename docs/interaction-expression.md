# Interaction Expression

**Phase:** IV.4B - Interaction Expression
**Status:** Documentation only - no implementation
**Foundation:** [`interaction-language.md`](interaction-language.md)
**Frozen dependencies:** Architecture, Narrative, Composition, Typography, Surface, Photography, Interaction Foundation

---

## Purpose

Interaction Expression translates the frozen Interaction Foundation into chapter-level behavioral identities.

The goal is not interaction variety. It is behavioral consistency through narrative difference. Each homepage chapter should behave differently because each chapter asks the visitor to do a different kind of thinking.

This document defines behavioral intent only. It does not define controls, animations, motion, timing, hover effects, microinteractions, JavaScript, CSS, Liquid, or implementation.

---

## Expression Principle

Every chapter receives one dominant behavioral relationship.

The relationship may be welcome and orientation, curiosity and discovery, inspection and evidence, trust and validation, reading and reflection, or utility and resolution. If behavior does not clarify the chapter's role, it does not belong in that chapter.

Interaction should follow the frozen narrative:

```
Orientation
  ->
Exploration
  ->
Inspection
  ->
Validation
  ->
Reflection
  ->
Verification
  ->
Identification
  ->
Resolution
  ->
Closure
  ->
Continuation
```

---

## Interaction Transitions

Interaction should move like a sequence of visitor state changes.

Each chapter inherits the confidence created by the chapter before it, then changes behavior just enough to answer the next uncertainty.

```
Hero
  Visitor becomes oriented.

Editorial Outcomes
  Visitor explores life-fit.

Ingredients
  Visitor inspects substance.

Community Confidence
  Visitor validates trust socially.

Formulation Philosophy
  Visitor reflects on intent.

Scientific Confidence
  Visitor verifies claims.

Human Proof
  Visitor identifies personally.

FAQ
  Visitor resolves objections.

Future Self
  Visitor reflects on commitment.

Footer
  Visitor exits or continues calmly.
```

Transitions should feel like behavioral momentum, not new interaction patterns. The interface should change its posture because the visitor's question has changed.

---

## Confidence Progression

Interaction reduces uncertainty across the page.

```
What is this?
  ->
Can this fit my life?
  ->
What is inside?
  ->
Do others trust it?
  ->
Was this thoughtfully made?
  ->
Can I verify it?
  ->
Can I see myself using it?
  ->
What still concerns me?
  ->
Am I ready?
  ->
Where do I go next?
```

Confidence should accumulate. Earlier chapters reduce recognition and relevance uncertainty. Middle chapters reduce evidence and trust uncertainty. Later chapters reduce personal, practical, and commitment uncertainty.

As confidence grows, behavior should become less insistent. The interface should not keep asking for attention after the visitor understands enough to continue.

## 1. Hero

### Behavioral Purpose

Hero behavior welcomes the visitor and removes first hesitation.

The visitor should immediately understand what the product is, what decision is available, and why the page feels trustworthy. Interaction should make the opening feel clear before it asks for commitment.

### Interaction Role

Orientation and confidence.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *what is this, and should I keep going?*

### Behavioral Tone

Direct, calm, confident, immediately legible.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Reveal only what supports first understanding. The Hero should not require exploration to become clear.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should reduce hesitation. The visitor should feel that every available path is understandable and stable.

### Interaction Energy

High clarity, low friction. The chapter may carry strong behavioral authority, but it should not feel busy.

### Interaction Silence

Silence protects first impression. Anything that does not help recognition, trust, or orientation should stay quiet.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography owns the promise. Interaction should help the visitor act on that promise without interrupting it.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography establishes product presence. Interaction should not compete with recognition.

---

## 2. Editorial Outcomes

### Behavioral Purpose

Editorial Outcomes behavior supports curiosity and gentle exploration.

The visitor should feel invited to understand how the product enters real life without being interrupted or pushed.

### Interaction Role

Exploration and reading support.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *how could this fit into my life?*

### Behavioral Tone

Warm, patient, observational.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should reward curiosity. Additional context may become available when it deepens understanding, but the chapter should remain readable without effort.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should feel supportive rather than transactional. It should confirm exploration without turning the chapter into a task.

### Interaction Energy

Gentle expansion. Interaction should invite the visitor to stay with the story.

### Interaction Silence

Silence protects narrative flow. Reading should not be interrupted by unnecessary behavioral prompts.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography carries transformation and outcome language. Interaction should support pacing and comprehension.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography makes the outcome believable. Interaction should let observed moments remain uninterrupted.

---

## 3. Ingredients

### Behavioral Purpose

Ingredients behavior supports inspection and evidence.

The visitor should feel that curiosity is rewarded with clarity. Interaction should make substance easier to understand without turning evidence into entertainment.

### Interaction Role

Inspection and informational confidence.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *what is inside, and can I understand it?*

### Behavioral Tone

Precise, clear, measured, confident.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Progressive disclosure is appropriate when it makes evidence more digestible. Critical substance should not be hidden, but deeper detail may unfold as curiosity increases.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should confirm what is being inspected and why it matters. The visitor should never lose the object of attention.

### Interaction Energy

Focused and responsive. This chapter may carry more behavioral activity because inspection is part of trust.

### Interaction Silence

Silence gives evidence authority. Behavior should pause when the visitor needs to read, compare, or absorb material detail.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography labels and explains. Interaction should make information easier to navigate conceptually, not louder.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography presents ingredients as evidence. Interaction should support inspection without turning objects into decoration.

---

## 4. Community Confidence

### Behavioral Purpose

Community Confidence behavior supports collective trust with minimal friction.

The visitor should feel that adoption is credible without being asked to perform belief.

### Interaction Role

Validation through low-pressure exploration.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *do people like me trust this enough to keep using it?*

### Behavioral Tone

Calm, social, credible, understated.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should support credibility, not spectacle. Collective proof should remain easy to absorb.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should reinforce trust quietly. It should not make proof feel like a performance.

### Interaction Energy

Moderate to low. The chapter should feel available, not insistent.

### Interaction Silence

Silence protects collective voice. Interaction should not over-explain what typography and proof already make clear.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography often owns this chapter. Interaction should yield to collective language.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography, when present, supports realness. Interaction should not make community feel staged.

---

## 5. Formulation Philosophy

### Behavioral Purpose

Formulation Philosophy behavior supports reflective reading.

The visitor should feel the brand thinking, not an interface performing. Interaction should slow down and let conviction emerge through words and pace.

### Interaction Role

Reflection and thoughtful progression.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *why is this approach different?*

### Behavioral Tone

Quiet, deliberate, intelligent.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should be minimal. The chapter should not hide its point of view behind behavior.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should be restrained and almost invisible. It should support orientation, not create emphasis.

### Interaction Energy

Low. This chapter should feel read, not operated.

### Interaction Silence

Silence is central. Behavioral quiet is not absence here; it is the way the chapter gives the manifesto enough authority to be considered.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography owns the argument. Interaction should protect the reading experience.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography supports craft and material honesty. Interaction should not disturb stillness.

---

## 6. Scientific Confidence

### Behavioral Purpose

Scientific Confidence behavior supports verification.

The visitor should feel that claims can be examined calmly and clearly.

### Interaction Role

Evidence, clarity, and controlled disclosure.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *can I verify the claims?*

### Behavioral Tone

Precise, controlled, accountable.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should clarify evidence in a controlled way. It may help sequence technical information, but it should never obscure what is essential to trust.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should confirm state and consequence with clarity. Ambiguity weakens scientific confidence.

### Interaction Energy

Focused and measured. Behavior may become more active when it helps verification, but it should remain calm.

### Interaction Silence

Silence gives technical information room to be trusted. Behavior should not dramatize evidence.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography explains process and standards. Interaction should make that explanation easier to follow.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography documents credibility. Interaction should support evidence, not theatricalize it.

---

## 7. Human Proof

### Behavioral Purpose

Human Proof behavior supports empathy and identification.

The visitor should feel close to a real person without feeling managed or manipulated.

### Interaction Role

Supportive identification.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *can I picture myself succeeding?*

### Behavioral Tone

Respectful, human, gentle, sincere.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should feel personal only when it deepens identification. It should not turn human proof into a persuasion device.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should be supportive and unobtrusive. The person should remain dignified.

### Interaction Energy

Low to moderate. Interaction should feel responsive to empathy, not eager for conversion.

### Interaction Silence

Silence protects dignity. The chapter should not over-handle personal proof.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography carries testimony or felt experience. Interaction should let the voice remain human.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography gives the person reality. Interaction should preserve intimacy and respect.

---

## 8. FAQ

### Behavioral Purpose

FAQ behavior resolves practical uncertainty with maximum efficiency.

The visitor should get answers quickly, without narrative friction or decorative behavior.

### Interaction Role

Utility and resolution.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *what is still stopping me?*

### Behavioral Tone

Fast, plain, calm, useful.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should be direct and utility-first. Information may be organized for clarity, but answers should not feel withheld.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should make state obvious. The visitor should always know what changed and where the answer lives.

### Interaction Energy

High efficiency, low emotion. FAQ may be behaviorally direct because utility owns the chapter.

### Interaction Silence

Silence removes friction. FAQ should feel fast because unnecessary behavior has been removed, not because the chapter is empty.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography owns the questions and answers. Interaction should make them easier to use.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography should usually disappear. Interaction should reinforce utility, not atmosphere.

---

## 9. Future Self

### Behavioral Purpose

Future Self behavior supports reflection and emotional closure.

The visitor should feel the page becoming quieter, not more demanding, as commitment becomes possible.

### Interaction Role

Reflection and calm commitment.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *what happens if I commit?*

### Behavioral Tone

Spacious, resolved, optimistic, restrained.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should be minimal. The emotional resolution should not be fragmented by unnecessary behavior.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should reassure without pressure. Commitment should feel calm.

### Interaction Energy

Reduced and focused. Behavior should support the closing decision without creating urgency.

### Interaction Silence

Silence is part of the close. The chapter should give the visitor room to feel resolution rather than asking for more attention.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography becomes concise. Interaction should not add verbal or behavioral noise.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography carries atmosphere. Interaction should protect that atmosphere.

---

## 10. Footer

### Behavioral Purpose

Footer behavior completes the page and preserves orientation.

The visitor should understand where they can continue without feeling persuaded again.

### Interaction Role

Completion and continuation.

### Visitor Intent

The visitor is asking: *where can I go next if I need to?*

### Behavioral Tone

Calm, final, useful, non-persuasive.

### Disclosure Philosophy

Disclosure should be limited to orientation and continuity. The footer should not reopen the sales argument.

### Feedback Philosophy

Feedback should be plain and reliable. The footer is a place for trust continuity, not behavioral drama.

### Interaction Energy

Low. The page is ending.

### Interaction Silence

Silence preserves closure. The footer should end the page calmly by avoiding any behavior that restarts persuasion.

### Relationship With Typography

Typography and navigation carry orientation. Interaction should remain practical.

### Relationship With Photography

Photography should not restart visual desire. Interaction should support quiet closure.

## Interaction Progression

Behavior evolves with the frozen homepage narrative:

```
Orientation
  ->
Exploration
  ->
Inspection
  ->
Validation
  ->
Reflection
  ->
Verification
  ->
Identification
  ->
Resolution
  ->
Closure
  ->
Continuation
```

The page begins by reducing hesitation, then invites exploration, rewards inspection, validates trust, slows into reflection, verifies claims, supports identification, resolves objections, quiets into commitment, and closes with orientation.

This progression prevents behavioral repetition. Hero should not behave like FAQ. Ingredients should not behave like Formulation Philosophy. Scientific Confidence should not behave like Human Proof. Future Self earns reduced interaction because the page has already built confidence.

---

## Behavioral Energy Progression

Interaction intensity should rise only when it improves decision clarity and fall when confidence has been earned:

```
Hero
  High clarity

Editorial Outcomes
  Gentle exploration

Ingredients
  Focused inspection

Community Confidence
  Low-friction validation

Formulation Philosophy
  Reflective quiet

Scientific Confidence
  Controlled verification

Human Proof
  Respectful support

FAQ
  Maximum efficiency

Future Self
  Reduced interaction

Footer
  Quiet continuation
```

Behavioral energy is not visual activity. The most active chapter may still feel calm if it reduces uncertainty. The quietest chapter may still be interactive if it gives the visitor exactly what they need.

Energy should never rise to create excitement for its own sake. It rises for inspection, verification, or utility; it falls for reflection, dignity, closure, and confidence.

---

## Interaction Consistency Rules

1. Behavior should follow narrative.
2. Every chapter must own one dominant interaction role.
3. Interaction should become quieter as confidence grows.
4. Feedback should always reduce uncertainty.
5. Behavior should never compete with reading.
6. Interaction should respect Typography and Photography ownership.
7. Every interaction must have narrative purpose.
8. Disclosure should clarify instead of creating suspense.
9. Utility chapters should prioritize speed over atmosphere.
10. Evidence chapters should reward inspection without dramatizing proof.
11. Human chapters should protect dignity.
12. Closing chapters should reduce behavioral pressure.
13. If behavior needs motion to be understood, the behavior is not clear enough.

## Definition Of Done

Interaction Expression is complete when every homepage chapter has a clear behavioral identity, every behavior supports the frozen narrative role, and the full sequence reads as one coherent PulseOps interaction language.

Future Interaction Systems work must build from this expression map rather than reopening chapter behavior identity.
