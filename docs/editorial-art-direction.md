## 1. Philosophy

### What makes an editorial Shopify homepage feel premium?

Premium DTC brands do not sell with sections. They sell with **atmosphere, sequence, and conviction**. A $400 theme must feel like a brand hired a creative studio — not like a merchant stacked twelve Dawn blocks and uploaded better photos.

Premium perception comes from:

- **One continuous story** — the visitor never wonders which “section” they entered; they wonder what happens next in the story.
- **Visual ownership** — every scroll beat has a single dominant element (product, person, photograph, typographic statement). Everything else defers.
- **Restraint** — fewer elements, larger gestures, quieter UI. Luxury is what you remove.
- **Intentional rhythm** — the page breathes, accelerates, pauses, and surprises. Monotony is the enemy of premium.
- **Evidence as editorial** — proof reads like a magazine fact-check, not a marketing dashboard.

PulseOps competes with AG1, Seed, Ritual, Momentous, Greenlight, and the best Shopify Theme Store flagships (Prestige, Concept, Impulse). Those brands win on **felt coherence** — every pixel belongs to the same world.

### Shopify sections vs editorial chapters

| Shopify sections | Editorial chapters |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Modular, interchangeable blocks | Sequential, irreplaceable story beats |
| Each section carries its own UI chrome | UI disappears; content and photography lead |
| Merchant thinks in “features” | Merchant thinks in “moments” |
| Repetition is invisible until scroll fatigue | Repetition breaks the illusion of a single brand world |
| Social proof, FAQ, and CTA feel like plugins | Proof, utility, and conversion feel like narrative roles |
| Background alternation = visual design | Surface shifts = emotional punctuation |

A section answers: *What content goes here?*  
A chapter answers: *What must the visitor feel before they continue?*

### Why premium brands feel like one continuous story

World-class homepages treat scroll as **time**, not distance.

The visitor enters with a question. Each chapter resolves exactly one question and opens the next. Photography, typography, and negative space carry the handoff — not divider lines and not “new section” padding jumps.

When it works:

- The visitor forgets they are on a product page.
- Proof never feels like repetition because each proof type has a **different visual grammar** (numbers vs philosophy vs lab vs portrait).
- Conversion feels earned — the finale is the emotional conclusion of everything above it.

When it fails:

- The same layout appears three times with different copy.
- Stars, stats, and badges accumulate like a landing page template.
- FAQ and CTA break the spell with generic Shopify geometry.

PulseOps art direction exists to prevent the second outcome — across every vertical, forever.

## 2. Visual Principles

Fifteen principles govern every PulseOps premium homepage. No chapter may violate more than one; zero is the target.

### 01 — One visual owner per chapter
Every chapter has a single dominant element: product, ritual photograph, flat-lay, typographic stat, manifesto image, lab scene, portrait, aspirational scene, or brand mark. If two elements compete, the chapter is not finished.

### 02 — One emotional objective per chapter
Visual treatment serves one feeling exit. A chapter cannot simultaneously shout “trust me” and “buy now.” Conversion intensity is reserved for Hero and Future Self.

### 03 — Photography leads; UI follows
The eye should land on light, texture, and human presence before borders, cards, or buttons. Chrome is a necessary evil, never a design strategy.

### 04 — Typography is the second photograph
When no image dominates (Community Confidence, FAQ), type *is* the visual. Scale, weight, and letter-spacing carry authority — not boxes around numbers.

### 05 — Whitespace is confidence
Premium brands leave room. Dense modules signal anxiety. Negative space is not “empty” — it is the pause between sentences.

### 06 — Rhythm must change every 1–2 chapters
If the last chapter was image-led split, the next must change grammar: full-bleed, typographic-only, portrait stage, documentary inset, or inverse finale. Same geometry twice in a row is a failure.

### 07 — Evidence should never feel like marketing
Stats without story feel like ads. Lab copy without craft feels like compliance. Reviews without a person feel like widgets. Art direction must humanize or typographize proof — never card-stack it.

### 08 — Restraint increases perceived price
Every removed badge, star row, and commitment strip raises the theme’s perceived value. When in doubt, subtract.

### 09 — Surfaces signal narrative shifts
Background tone changes are emotional punctuation — ivory to cool neutral to warm documentary to deep inverse finale. Arbitrary banding is forbidden; every shift answers “what changed in the story?”

### 10 — Motion supports story; never decorates
Movement draws the eye to what matters this chapter. Motion that exists because “premium themes animate” is cheap. Stillness is also a motion choice.

### 11 — Mobile is the first composition
Art direction is approved on mobile first. Desktop adds asymmetry and scale — it does not introduce new layout families the phone never saw.

### 12 — Merchant photography must look inevitable
Demo photography is not placeholder — it is the standard. Cropping, lighting, and subject matter teach merchants what “on-brand” means. Bad merchant uploads should look like the exception, not the rule.

### 13 — Conversion is role-based, not everywhere
Primary buttons appear where intent is high. Mid-page chapters use quiet links or no CTA. Button color repetition across the page is Theme Store mediocrity.

### 14 — Utility must look subordinate
FAQ, shipping notes, and legal reassurance are whispered — never shouted. Utility that looks like a chapter headline steals from the finale.

### 15 — The finale is emotional, not promotional
Future Self is not a coupon block. It is the visitor seeing who they become. Art direction must close the story before it asks for the click.

## 4. Editorial Rhythm

### How a premium homepage should scroll

Think in **beats**, not sections. A beat is one emotional inhalation or exhalation.

**Ideal macro rhythm (supplement and wellness verticals):**

```
LARGE  →  Hero product
WARM   →  Transformation lifestyle
RICH   →  Ingredients flat-lay
QUIET  →  Community typographic breath
STATEMENT → Philosophy manifesto
COOL   →  Scientific documentary
INTIMATE → Human portrait
FINALE → Future Self aspiration
WHISPER → FAQ utility
CLOSE  → Footer brand
```

**Alternate valid patterns by vertical:**

- **Skincare:** Product → Skin ritual → Ingredient macro → Clinical → Before/after restraint → Dermatologist voice → User portrait → Finale
- **Coffee:** Product → Morning ritual → Origin flat-lay → Community → Roasting philosophy → Sourcing documentary → Barista portrait → Subscribe finale
- **Electronics:** Product → Capability story → Component macro → Reviews data → Engineering philosophy → Testing lab → Creator portrait → Buy finale

### Rhythm rules

| Rule | Target |
|------|--------|
| **Rhythm change frequency** | Every 1–2 chapters — never three consecutive chapters with same layout family |
| **Breathing room** | Intentional handoffs: ~2.5–4rem mobile between chapters; not 6rem+ dead air |
| **Surprise frequency** | One surprise every 3–4 chapters — unexpected scale, full-bleed break, typographic-only beat |
| **Image vs type alternation** | Never more than two image-dominant chapters without a typographic or utility breath |
| **Density wave** | High (Ingredients) must be followed by low (Community Confidence) — density waves prevent fatigue |
| **Finale elevation** | Future Self must feel visually louder than FAQ and footer — not louder than Hero |

### Predictability is death

If a visitor can guess the next chapter’s layout before it arrives, rhythm has failed. The narrative question can be predictable; the **visual grammar** must not be.

## 5. Motion Direction

Motion is **narrative punctuation**, not decoration. Principles only — no implementation spec.

### Global motion rules

- **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`** — every motion choice has a still fallback.
- **One motion type per chapter** — never combine parallax, loop, and stagger in the same beat.
- **Motion stops for reading** — when quote or manifesto copy exceeds 40 words visible, image motion pauses or stays still.
- **No motion on utility** — FAQ, footer, and verification lines are always still.
- **Looping motion photography** — only Hero, Future Self, or full-bleed atmospheric chapters; max 8–12 second loop, imperceptible seam.

### Per-chapter motion matrix

| Chapter | Motion level | Allowed | Forbidden |
|---------|--------------|---------|-----------|
| **Hero** | Subtle | Product shimmer, slow light shift, gentle float | Auto-playing video with sound, particle effects |
| **Transformation** | Subtle | Fade-up copy, still or Ken Burns lifestyle | Carousel, parallax on split |
| **Ingredients** | None to micro | Hover scale on matrix thumbs | Parallax flat-lay, rotating ingredients |
| **Community Confidence** | None to subtle | One-time count-up on numerals | Looping backgrounds, swipe animations |
| **Formulation Philosophy** | Subtle | Text stagger, still manifesto image | Video manifesto unless vertical demands |
| **Scientific Confidence** | Subtle | Slow pan on wide documentary frame | Animated icons, pulsing badges |
| **Human Proof** | None to subtle | Quote crossfade, still portrait | Auto-rotate faster than 8s, kinetic dots |
| **Future Self** | Subtle to looping | Atmospheric loop behind still CTA | Urgency pulse, flashing price |
| **FAQ** | Micro only | Accordion expand | Scroll-triggered fades per item |
| **Footer** | None | — | Any motion |

### When motion should stop

- User focuses input or opens accordion
- Reduced motion preference is on
- Mobile low-power or slow connection (future consideration)
- Any chapter whose job is **reading** — Philosophy, Human Proof quote, Scientific process copy

### When motion must never compete with reading

- Pull quotes longer than two lines
- Manifesto body copy visible alongside image
- FAQ answers expanded
- Price and offer line in finale — CTA must be the calmest element in view

## 6. Photography Direction

Photography is the primary brand voice. PulseOps demos must teach merchants what excellence looks like.

### Genre by chapter

| Chapter | Primary genre | Secondary |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Hero | Studio product | Environmental product |
| Transformation | Lifestyle editorial | Environmental portrait |
| Ingredients | Flat-lay editorial | Macro ingredient |
| Community Confidence | None or documentary crowd texture | — |
| Formulation Philosophy | Cinematic still life | Process abstract |
| Scientific Confidence | Documentary industrial | Lab detail macro |
| Human Proof | Portrait editorial | Intimate lifestyle |
| Future Self | Aspirational lifestyle | Product in ritual |
| FAQ | None | — |
| Footer | None | — |

### Lighting

| Genre | Lighting direction |
|-------|-------------------|
| Studio product | Controlled key + soft fill; defined edge; no harsh drop shadow cliché |
| Lifestyle | Natural window or golden-hour; directional warmth |
| Flat-lay | Overhead soft box; even with gentle falloff; tactile shadows |
| Documentary | Available light; cool neutral; authenticity over polish |
| Portrait | Soft directional; eye catchlight; shallow depth implied |
| Macro | Diffused side light; reveal texture |

### Color temperature

| Chapter zone | Temperature |
|--------------|-------------|
| Hero → Transformation | Warm neutral (4800–5500K feel) |
| Ingredients | Neutral to slightly warm — food/material accurate |
| Community → Philosophy | Neutral |
| Scientific Confidence | Cool neutral (5500–6500K feel) |
| Human Proof | Warm intimate (4500–5200K feel) |
| Future Self | Warm aspirational or brand-inverse contrast |

### Cropping philosophy

- **Hero product:** Full packaging legible; never cropped below brand mark.
- **Lifestyle:** Leave narrative space for copy — subject not dead-center unless mobile full-bleed.
- **Flat-lay:** 10–15% breathing margin; no cramped ingredient cram.
- **Documentary:** Wide establishing + optional detail inset — never cheesy scientist close-up.
- **Portrait:** Headroom intentional; eyes on upper third; hands/routine objects may enter frame.
- **Never:** Stock smiles, pointing at product, exaggerated before/after, clip-art supplements.

### Merchant media guidance

Use this table when preparing or replacing chapter photography. Upload assets that match the chapter’s **recommended aspect ratio** and leave **safe negative space** where copy will sit — especially Future Self.

| Chapter role | Recommended aspect ratio | Safe-space notes |
|--------------|-------------------------|------------------|
| Hero product | 4:5 or 1:1 | Full label legible; product centered with margin |
| Lifestyle split (Transformation) | 4:5 | Leave narrative space for copy; subject off dead-center |
| Ingredient flat-lay | 3:2 or 4:3 | 10–15% breathing margin; no cramped edges |
| Documentary / lab (Scientific) | 16:9 or 3:2 | Wide establishing; cool neutral tone |
| Human proof portrait | 4:5 | Headroom intentional; eyes on upper third |
| **Future Self cinematic CTA** | **16:9 minimum; ideally 21:9** | **30–40% safe negative space for text (typically lower-left); keep couple, horizon, and product away from edges** |

**Never upload images with baked-in text, buttons, badges, or icons.** Marketing copy belongs in the theme, not burned into photography.

**Future Self images** need deliberate safe space. Main subjects (people, product, horizon) should sit inside the frame — not flush against top, bottom, or side edges — so cinematic cropping can preserve the composition on desktop and mobile.

### Photography packs (future)

Each vertical pack ships a **coherent photo suite** — same lighting family, same casting logic, same color grade. Merchants replace images; they should not need to replace the creative direction.

## 7. Typography Direction

Typography carries chapters when photography steps back. The scroll should feel like changing **type voice**, not just changing font size.

### Evolution through the scroll

| Scroll phase | Type character |
|--------------|----------------|
| Opening (Hero) | Expressive — largest scale, shortest copy |
| Relevance (Transformation) | Confident — statement headline, supportive body |
| Substance (Ingredients) | Documentary — captions, labels, matrix quiet |
| Scale (Community) | Expressive numerals — quiet everything else |
| Conviction (Philosophy) | Manifesto — wide measure, statement weight |
| Authority (Scientific) | Documentary — precise, cool, no flourish |
| Emotion (Human Proof) | Emotional — quote dominant, attribution whisper |
| Commitment (Future Self) | Confident minimal — one promise, quiet price |
| Utility (FAQ) | Quiet — system size, no display type |
| Close (Footer) | Quiet — navigational, small, stable |

### When headings grow

- Hero — maximum display
- Human Proof quote — second-largest expressive moment on page
- Community Confidence numerals — scale as image substitute
- Future Self — confident not gigantic; restraint signals premium

### When headings shrink

- Ingredients matrix — captions lead
- Scientific process labels — precision over display
- FAQ — questions are body-scale
- Footer — no display headings

### When body copy should disappear

- Community Confidence — one sentence maximum; numbers speak
- Human Proof — quote only; no explanatory paragraph
- Future Self — offer line + reassurance only
- Footer — no marketing body copy

### Rules

- One display moment per chapter — never headline + subhead + pull quote all competing
- Eyebrows are always caption scale — they label, never shout
- Line length: display type short (8–16 words); body max ~65 characters comfortable measure
- Vertical packs may swap serif/sans roles; hierarchy rules do not change

## 8. Theme Store Standard

### Benchmarks

PulseOps competes with **brand sites** (AG1, Seed, Ritual, Momentous, Greenlight) and **Theme Store flagships** (Prestige, Concept, Impulse). The merchant is comparing screenshots in the first five seconds and memory in the first five minutes.

### Within five seconds a merchant should notice

- **This is one story** — not a section stack
- **Photography is doing the selling** — not cards and badges
- **Typography feels magazine-grade** — scale and restraint
- **The demo knows my vertical** — supplement, skincare, coffee, etc. without feeling templated
- **I could not build this in Dawn alone** — not without months of agency work

### After five minutes a merchant should remember

- **The chapter sequence makes sense** — they could explain the narrative to their team
- **One moment stood out** — portrait, flat-lay, lab, or finale (varies by visitor)
- **It feels expensive** — even if they cannot articulate why
- **Settings seem curated** — presets, not panic toggles
- **They trust PulseOps for their category** — “this theme gets wellness / beauty / coffee”

### What separates $400 from $180

| $180 theme | $400 PulseOps target |
|------------|---------------------|
| Many sections, many options | Fewer chapters, better presets |
| Generic social proof blocks | Human Proof as editorial portrait |
| FAQ as afterthought | Utility whisper |
| CTA as gradient card | Future Self as emotional finale |
| Stock photo placeholders | Directional photography packs |
| Same layout repeated | Rhythm-changing visual grammars |

## 9. Future Vertical Packs

Art direction is **vertical-adaptive, philosophy-constant**. Chapters 01–10 do not change. Visual expression does.

### Adaptation model

Each vertical pack defines:

1. **Photography suite** — genre mapping per chapter with vertical subjects
2. **Color temperature arc** — warm/cool journey appropriate to category
3. **Typography tone** — serif-forward (beauty, supplement) vs sans-forward (electronics, fitness) without breaking hierarchy rules
4. **Manifesto voice** — what “philosophy” means in category (roasting, formulation, engineering, training)
5. **Documentary idiom** — lab for supplements, clinic for skincare, roastery for coffee, wind tunnel for fitness gear

### Vertical sketches

| Vertical | Hero owner | Transformation | Substance chapter | Documentary chapter | Human Proof |
|----------|------------|----------------|-------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| **Skincare** | Serum bottle | Morning skin ritual | Ingredient macro | Clinical testing | User portrait, bare-skin confidence |
| **Coffee** | Bag + cup | Morning kitchen ritual | Origin flat-lay | Roasting / sourcing | Regular at café |
| **Fitness** | Equipment or apparel | Training moment | Material / tech macro | Performance testing | Athlete portrait |
| **Electronics** | Device hero | Desk / creative workflow | Component detail | Engineering / QA | Creator using product |
| **Pets** | Product + pet context | Pet ritual moment | Ingredient / nutrition flat-lay | Safety testing | Pet parent lifestyle |
| **Creator** | Tool / camera | Studio workflow | Feature detail macro | Build quality | Creator portrait |

### What never changes across verticals

- Ten-chapter narrative arc
- One question per chapter
- One visual owner per chapter
- Rhythm change every 1–2 chapters
- Hero + Future Self as only primary conversion peaks
- Evidence without marketing chrome
- FAQ subordinate, footer brand-owned
